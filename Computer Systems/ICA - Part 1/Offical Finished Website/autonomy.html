AUTONOMY

One of the largest problems with autonomy in general, not just driverless cars, is the fact that jobs will be filled by machines instead of humans and a shift happens where people are the caretakers of these machines instead of the operators.

With driverless cars this problem is multi-faceted: Safety concerns, if the car is fully autonomous does this mean that someone should be allowed and possibly even encouraged to do other things on their journey or should they still have to be mindful of what is occurring during?

Decimation of entire job markets, once driverless vehicles become the norm; transportation will be completely changed and there’ll be no need for taxis/ trains/ coaches anymore as the driver becomes completely defunct.

Changing how we travel entirely, possibly the largest change of all is how the individual will travel. It is proposed that in 20 years’ time people won’t need to learn to drive, but is this taking away their freedom – as surely the data from their journeys will be recorded and will people even have their own vehicles or will a company essentially have control of their travel?
